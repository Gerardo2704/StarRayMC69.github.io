    Result of the Berlin Conference

    What is the Berlin Conference?
    The Berlin Conference of 1884–1885, also known as the Congo Conference or West Africa Conference, regulated European colonization in Africa during the New Imperialism period and coincided with Germany's sudden emergence as an imperial power.

    The conference contributed to ushering in a period of heightened colonial activity by European powers, which eliminated or overrode most existing forms of African autonomy and self-governance.

    Outcome of the Berlin Conference
    The Berlin Conference was intended to reduce the conflict between European Nations and discard the slave trade, but ultimately divided up Africa to the European Nations.

    The General Act of the Berlin Conference, formalized the Scramble for Africa. The diplomats in Berlin laid the rules of competition by which the great powers were to be guided in seeking colonies. No nation was to stake claims in Africa without notifying other powers of its intentions.

    In 1884, Otto von Bismarck convened the Berlin Conference to discuss the African problem. Its outcome, the General Act of the Berlin Conference, formalized the Scramble for Africa. The diplomats in Berlin laid the rules of competition by which the great powers were to be guided in seeking colonies.

    Philippines: Surge in Child Abuse Cases Amidst a Pandemic

    Clarence Pagaduan
    November 26, 2020

    A report from the Philippines Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) shows a sudden spike in child abuse, both violent and sexual, in relation to the COVID-19 quarantine.



    In many Third World countries, violence against children continues to be a pressing problem, especially during this on-going COVID-19 pandemic. Such violence is often manifested in the form of abuse. The Philippines is similar to many other Asian countries in the way this type of domestic violence is observed.

    Despite the increasing awareness of children's rights and the protection of children, there continue to be several reported cases of child abuse in Asian countries like the Philippines. In the Philippines, there are approximately 40 million children under the age of 18 and according to several reports, there were over 300,000 cases of child exploitation and online abuse in the Philippines, said the DoJ, using data from the U.S.-based National Center for Missing and Exploited Children. While there were only 100,000 cases during this timeframe in 2019. Of these cases, 44.5% were classified as sexual abuse, 21.67% as physical abuse, and 15.07% as neglect.

    Planning Officer Aple Shane Lomoljo said that in a study conducted by the Council for the Welfare of Children (CWC), the perpetrators of the abuse are usually housemates and family members of the children. Increasing risks of violence where the children are exposed include maltreatment, gender-based violence, and sexual exploitation. Lomoljo said that what increases the likelihood that the children are physically, psychologically, or sexually abused is that some of the parents are distressed in losing their jobs because of the pandemic, and they tend to take it out on their children.

    She said child abuse causes the children to be emotional, as it changes the way on how the child sees him/herself, how he decides for him/herself, and how he/she perceives other people.

    The DSWD is highly requesting any witnesses of such cases to report it immediately.

    Chinese Family Values

    The Love of a Child to their Parents

    “The family is one of nature's masterpieces”, a man once said. Family is something that upholds the values and morals that people have, and they have been a part of daily life for many centuries. It is said that a parent’s love for their child is stronger than anything in the universe, but a child’s love can be just as strong. China has been through several dynasties for over 4000 years and has been exploited several times by foreign countries, despite all this, one value still remains to this day, the love of a family.

    Have you ever heard of the tale of Mulan? According to the legend, Mulan takes her aged father's place in the conscription for the army by disguising herself as a man. This story sets a good example of the love that strengthens and sets Chinese families aside from the rest. Unlike modern families today where the ‘hierarchy’ is evenly distributed between the elders and the parents, Chinese traditional families had roles based on gender and age and used to be very patriarchal, this meant that it was very emphasized that the male in the family, typically the oldest, be the head of the house, and take the responsibility of protecting their families, while women, on the other hand, have the responsibility of taking care of the house, the children, and bring honor to the family.

    Of the 8 most important values in Chinese traditional culture, filial piety is one of them. Filial piety is a virtue of respect for one's parents, elders, and ancestors. When children demonstrate filial piety in China, they are viewed as trustworthy and respectable. But how did these family values come to be such an important part of the Chinese traditional culture?

    In China, the family is largely understood through Confucian thought. In Confucian thinking, the family contains the most important relationships for individuals and forms the foundations of all social organization. This is in accordance with filial piety, the Confucian tenet that stresses the importance of age. The Chinese philosopher Confucius is most responsible for making filial piety a pivotal part of society. He described filial piety and argued for its importance in creating a peaceful family and society in his book, "Xiao Jing," also known as the "Classic of Filial Piety" which was written in the 4th century BCE.

    Sarsuwela sa Kamalayan ng Isang Pilipino


    Ang sarsuwela ay isang uri ng dulang komedya o melodrama na mayroong awit at kantahan, katulad ng isang ‘musical play’. Mula nang ito’y ipinakilala, lubos itong nakaapekto sa kamalayan, katalinhaga, at pagiging malikhain ng Pilipino, at ito ay talagang nagpapakita na ang mga Pilipino ay kakaiba kumpara sa ibang tao.

    Ang sarsuwela, bagama’t pinakilala noong panahon ng mga Kastila ay lubos na namulaklak ng panahon ng mga Amerikano. Ang zarzuela ay naging isang "makapangyarihang paraan" ng pagpapahayag ng nasyonalismong Pilipino sa panahon ng Pagsakop sa Espanya ng Pilipinas na sumunod sa tatlong siglo ng pamamahala ng Espanya. Masasalamin natin sa mga ito ang mga nangyare sa nakaraan (kasaysayan), mga damdamin ng mga Pilipino, at kung paano namumuhay ang mga tao. Isang mahusay at magaling na halimabawa ng sarsuwela ay ang sarsuwelang “Walang Sugat” na sinulat ng Severino Reyes noong 1898. Sa dulang ito, ang pangunahing tauhan na si Tenyong, ay napilitang iwanan ang kanyang kasintahan at magiging asawa na si Julia dahil kailangan pumunta sa isang labanan laban sa mga Kastila. Hindi man direkta, napapakita pa rin nito ang historya o kasaysayan ng Pilipino sa pananakop ng mga Kastila.

    Sa pamamagitan ng mga sarsuwelang ito ay napapakita ng mga aktor sa sarsuwela ang mga pangyayari na naganap sa iba’t ibang rehiyon at lugar sa bansa at nabibigyan nila ang mga ibang Pilipino ng dagdag kaalaman tungkol sa kultura at pamumuhay ng isang rehiyon, at dahil dito napapalawak nila ang kultura natin.

    Ito’y lubhasang nakaimpluwensya sa mga tao sapagkat pinapakita nito ang pagiging magaling at malikhain ng mga Pilipino sa mga patulang uri ng aliwan.

    Describe a reverse or thrust fault

    Reverse dip-slip faults result from horizontal compressional forces caused by a shortening, or contraction, of Earth's crust. The hanging wall moves up and over the footwall. Thrust faults are reverse faults that dip less than 45°.

    What causes its formation?

    Thrust and Reverse faults form by horizontal compressive stresses and so cause shortening of the crust. Because the hanging wall moves up relative to the footwall, most of these faults place older rocks over younger rocks. Younger over older relations can occur when previously .deformed rocks are thrust faulted.

    Types of Seismic Waves

    1. P-wave (Primary Wave)
      • This is the fastest kind of seismic wave, and, consequently, the first to 'arrive' at a seismic station. The P wave can move through solid rock and fluids, like water or the liquid layers of the earth. It pushes and pulls the rock it moves through just like sound waves push and pull the air.
        • Fun fact: Sometimes animals can hear the P waves of an earthquake. Dogs, for instance, commonly begin barking hysterically just before an earthquake 'hits' (or more specifically, before the surface waves arrive).

    2. S-wave (Secondary Wave)
      • This is the second wave you feel in an earthquake. It is slower than a P wave and can only move through solid rock, not through any liquid medium. S waves move rock particles up and down, or side-to-side--perpendicular to the direction that the wave is traveling in (the direction of wave propagation).
        • Fun fact: The property of S waves which states that they can only move through solids led seismologists to conclude that the Earth's outer core is a liquid.

    3. L-wave (Love Wave)
      • The first kind of surface wave is called a Love wave, a surface wave is of a lower frequency than body waves, and is easily distinguished on a seismogram as a result, they can only travel through the crust. A love wave is the fastest surface wave and moves the ground from side-to-side. Confined to the surface of the crust, Love waves produce entirely horizontal motion.
        • Fun fact: The love wave was named after A.E.H. Love, a British mathematician who worked out the mathematical model for this kind of wave in 1911.

    4. R-wave (Rayleigh Wave)
      • A Rayleigh wave rolls along the ground just like a wave rolls across a lake or an ocean. Because it rolls, it moves the ground up and down, and side-to-side in the same direction that the wave is moving. Most of the shaking felt from an earthquake is due to the Rayleigh wave, which can be much larger than the other waves.
        • Fun fact: This type of wave was named for John William Strutt, Lord Rayleigh, who mathematically predicted the existence of this kind of wave in 1885

    Why are aftershocks more dangerous than the main quake?

    • Aftershocks can be more dangerous as they are very unpredictable (they may not even occur until a few days or weeks later) and can destroy/collapse structures that have been damaged by the main shock

    • Some aftershocks may have a magnitude just as high as the main earthquake. There may also be some cases where an aftershock is so strong, that they may even be stronger than the main quake itself.

    5s, PPE and First Aid Rules

    5s

    1. Sort/Seiri
      Take out unnecessary items and dispose. Keep only the items you need at work, and discard or store everything else.

    2. Systematize/Seiton
      It means that there is a place for everything and everything should be in its place. Arrange necessary items in good order for use.

    3. Shine/Seiso
      At the end of each working day, take time to clean up your office or working space. Keep the workplace neat and clean.

    4. Standardize/Seiketsu
      Maintain high standard of cleaning and workshop organization at all times.

    5. Sustain/Shitsuke
      Do things spontaneously without being told or ordered. Self-discipline is a condition of training people to follow cleaning disciplines independently.

    Personal Protective Equipment and Clothing (PPE)

    PPE can protect you from hazards associated with jobs such as handling chemicals or working in a noisy environment. In food preparation this is the cooking outfit which gives protection to the worker against different hazards that may be encountered during cooking.

    These are the following

    • Hair covering/ hairnet – prevents hair from falling into food product.
    • Facial mask – barrier to airborne contamination during sneezing, coughing and talking.
    • Aprons – reduce risk of contamination and help maintain cleanliness.
    • Gloves – reduce risks of contamination.
    • Pot holder – protects against burns when taking hot items on top of the stove.

    First Aid Rules

    Burns - Cool the burn with cool water. Do not put grease, or oil on burn – they can make it worse. Do not try to clean a burn or break blisters. Call a physician.

    Falls - Stop severe bleeding. Cover wounds with sterile dressing. Keep the person comfortable and warm. If you think the bone is broken, do not move the person unless necessary as in the event of fire, call for a medical assistance.

    Poisoning - Swallowed Poison. If the container is available, use antidote recommended in the label. If none is given call the emergency station of a hospital, the nearest clinic, or rural health center. Tell them what kind of poison was taken and they will recommend an antidote. Do not try to neutralize a poison by giving raw eggs, salt water, mustard, vinegar or citrus fruit juices as an antidote or to cause vomiting. Never attempt to induce vomiting by sticking your fingers anywhere in the patient's mouth; this procedure can be very dangerous.
