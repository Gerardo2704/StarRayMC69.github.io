Headline or Title of the report COVID-19: UAE hosting vaccine trial for Sputnik V is good news
Byline (name of the reporter) By Hina Navin, October 14, 2020 15:10
Summary Lead
(This contains most of the 5Ws and 1H)		 The UAE has been chosen for Russian and Chinese vaccine trials. Only one vaccine has been approved so far, Sputnik V, which was approved by Russia’s health regulators on August 11. The UAE this week announced it would host phase III trials for this vaccine, the second trial after the emirates conducted trials for a Chinese vaccine.

Another big advantage is diverse demography of the UAE, home to people from every part of the globe. The two trials demonstrate global health experts’ faith in the UAE institutions’ ability to maintain integrity of collecting and processing scientific data.
Facts or details arranged from the most important to the least important
  • The UAE has been chosen for vaccine trials of the Sputnik V vaccine and Chinese vaccines because the country has world-class, health care infrastructure.
  • Sputnik V uses adenoviral vectors human adenoviruses that have had their genetic material removed that trick the recipient’s body into producing immune response.
  • Countries hosting trials will have a clear advantage in getting early access to the vaccine.
  • 31,000 volunteers, including the UAE’s top health officials have received the Chinese vaccine as part of phase III trials.
  • The Sputnik V vaccine is named after Sputnik 1, the first artificial Earth satellite launched in 1957.



Philippines: Surge in Child Abuse Cases Amidst a Pandemic

Clarence Pagaduan
November 26, 2020

A report from the Philippines Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) shows a sudden spike in child abuse, both violent and sexual, in relation to the COVID-19 quarantine.



In many Third World countries, violence against children continues to be a pressing problem, especially during this on-going COVID-19 pandemic. Such violence is often manifested in the form of abuse. The Philippines is similar to many other Asian countries in the way this type of domestic violence is observed.

Despite the increasing awareness of children's rights and the protection of children, there continue to be several reported cases of child abuse in Asian countries like the Philippines. In the Philippines, there are approximately 40 million children under the age of 18 and according to several reports, there were over 300,000 cases of child exploitation and online abuse in the Philippines, said the DoJ, using data from the U.S.-based National Center for Missing and Exploited Children. While there were only 100,000 cases during this timeframe in 2019. Of these cases, 44.5% were classified as sexual abuse, 21.67% as physical abuse, and 15.07% as neglect.

Planning Officer Aple Shane Lomoljo said that in a study conducted by the Council for the Welfare of Children (CWC), the perpetrators of the abuse are usually housemates and family members of the children. Increasing risks of violence where the children are exposed include maltreatment, gender-based violence, and sexual exploitation. Lomoljo said that what increases the likelihood that the children are physically, psychologically, or sexually abused is that some of the parents are distressed in losing their jobs because of the pandemic, and they tend to take it out on their children.

She said child abuse causes the children to be emotional, as it changes the way on how the child sees him/herself, how he decides for him/herself, and how he/she perceives other people.

The DSWD is highly requesting any witnesses of such cases to report it immediately.

Chinese Family Values

The Love of a Child to their Parents

“The family is one of nature's masterpieces”, a man once said. Family is something that upholds the values and morals that people have, and they have been a part of daily life for many centuries. It is said that a parent’s love for their child is stronger than anything in the universe, but a child’s love can be just as strong. China has been through several dynasties for over 4000 years and has been exploited several times by foreign countries, despite all this, one value still remains to this day, the love of a family.

Have you ever heard of the tale of Mulan? According to the legend, Mulan takes her aged father's place in the conscription for the army by disguising herself as a man. This story sets a good example of the love that strengthens and sets Chinese families aside from the rest. Unlike modern families today where the ‘hierarchy’ is evenly distributed between the elders and the parents, Chinese traditional families had roles based on gender and age and used to be very patriarchal, this meant that it was very emphasized that the male in the family, typically the oldest, be the head of the house, and take the responsibility of protecting their families, while women, on the other hand, have the responsibility of taking care of the house, the children, and bring honor to the family.

Of the 8 most important values in Chinese traditional culture, filial piety is one of them. Filial piety is a virtue of respect for one's parents, elders, and ancestors. When children demonstrate filial piety in China, they are viewed as trustworthy and respectable. But how did these family values come to be such an important part of the Chinese traditional culture?

In China, the family is largely understood through Confucian thought. In Confucian thinking, the family contains the most important relationships for individuals and forms the foundations of all social organization. This is in accordance with filial piety, the Confucian tenet that stresses the importance of age. The Chinese philosopher Confucius is most responsible for making filial piety a pivotal part of society. He described filial piety and argued for its importance in creating a peaceful family and society in his book, "Xiao Jing," also known as the "Classic of Filial Piety" which was written in the 4th century BCE.
